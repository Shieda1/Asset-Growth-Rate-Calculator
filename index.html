What is the asset growth rate?
The asset growth rate is a measurement of the period of time that the company has been growing it's assets. Simply it is the change in assets over a specific period of time and it is expressed as a percentage How to calculate asset growth rate?
To calculate asset growth rate you should use the following formula:
asset growth rate = (later price point - early price point) / early price point
example: company x's stock was priced at $112 per share 6 years ago and now the stock's price is $140, calculate the asset growth rate?
Using the asset growth rate formula the answer will be:
asset growth rate = (later price point - early price point) / early price point
asset growth rate = ($140 - $112) / $112
asset growth rate = 25%
Why use asset growth rate?
The asset growth rate is used to predict the future stock returns after taking into account the book to market ratio, momentum and the company's size.
